Ho Chi Minh City Adventure

Discover the vibrant energy and rich culture of Vietnam's largest city

Welcome to Saigon!

Ho Chi Minh City, still commonly referred to as Saigon, is a dynamic metropolis that never sleeps. From its French colonial architecture to bustling street markets and vibrant nightlife, Saigon offers endless opportunities for fun and adventure.

Street Food Tour

Embark on a culinary adventure through Saigon's famous street food scene. Try pho, banh mi, fresh spring rolls, and more from local vendors. Don't miss the night markets for an authentic taste of Vietnam!

Must-try: Banh xeo (Vietnamese pancake) at Banh Xeo 46A

Motorbike City Tour

Experience Saigon like a local on the back of a motorbike. Zip through hidden alleyways, visit local markets, and see all the major landmarks with an expert guide.

Hot tip: Evening tours let you see the city lights and experience the cooler temperatures.

Mekong Delta Day Trip

Escape the city for a day and explore the lush waterways of the Mekong Delta. Visit floating markets, sample tropical fruits, and learn about rural Vietnamese life.

Pro tip: Book a small group tour for a more personal experience.

Historical Exploration

Dive into Vietnam's history at the War Remnants Museum, Reunification Palace, and Cu Chi Tunnels. These sites offer powerful insights into the country's past.

Note: Some exhibits can be quite graphic but are important to see.

Rooftop Bar Hopping

Saigon's skyline comes alive at night. Visit famous rooftop bars like Chill Skybar or Saigon Saigon for cocktails with breathtaking views of the city.

Dress code: Most upscale rooftop bars enforce a smart casual dress code.

Market Shopping

Haggle for souvenirs at Ben Thanh Market or explore the less touristy Binh Tay Market in Chinatown. Find everything from silk scarves to Vietnamese coffee.

Bargaining tip: Start at about 50% of the asking price and meet in the middle.

Travel Tips for Ho Chi Minh City

  • Best time to visit: December to April (dry season)
  • Getting around: Grab (Southeast Asia's Uber) is affordable and reliable
  • Currency: Vietnamese Dong (VND) - carry small bills for street vendors
  • Crossing streets: Walk slowly and steadily - motorbikes will flow around you
  • Local etiquette: Dress modestly when visiting temples and pagodas
