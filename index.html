The Story of Saigon

From its humble beginnings to the vibrant metropolis it is today

Ho Chi Minh City, still affectionately known as Saigon, is a city with a rich and complex history. Originally a small fishing village inhabited by Khmer people, the area was conquered by the Vietnamese in the 17th century and grew into an important trading port.

Under French colonial rule from the mid-19th century, Saigon became known as the "Pearl of the Far East" for its beautiful architecture and cosmopolitan culture. The city was the capital of South Vietnam during the Vietnam War and witnessed some of the conflict's most pivotal moments, including the fall of Saigon in 1975 when it was renamed Ho Chi Minh City.

Today, the city is Vietnam's economic powerhouse, where gleaming skyscrapers stand alongside colonial-era buildings and ancient pagodas. This fascinating blend of old and new makes Ho Chi Minh City one of Southeast Asia's most captivating destinations.
